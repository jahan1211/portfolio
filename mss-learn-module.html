Why it matters · کیوں ضروری ہے
Improper disposal of menstrual pads can spread infection and cause environmental harm. Following these simple steps keeps you and your community safe.
Steps · اقدامات
1
Wrap the used pad in newspaper or old paper before disposing.
استعمال شدہ پیڈ کو اخبار میں لپیٹیں
2
Place in a covered dustbin — never in the toilet or open ground.
ڈھکے ہوئے ڈبے میں ڈالیں — بیت الخلاء میں نہیں
3
Wash hands with soap immediately after disposal.
فوری طور پر صابن سے ہاتھ دھوئیں

Design Decisions — Learn Module
Every choice is directly tied to a requirement from the MSS project document.
1
Progress Rings on Learn Home
Circular progress indicators on each topic card show completion %. Motivates users to continue. Users see exactly where they left off — no memory needed.
→ Req 3.3.4: Education module with tracking
2
TTS Player at Top of Article
The "Listen in Urdu" audio player appears before article text. Low-literacy users immediately see the audio option without having to scroll. Green sage color for audio = calm and accessible.
→ Req 3.3.3: AI voice-based guidance for low-literacy
3
Bilingual Everything (EN + UR)
Every heading, step, and UI label has an Urdu translation directly below it. Users who can't read English still understand context from Urdu. No switching needed.
→ Req 1.5 Obj.1: Multilingual content delivery
4
Video: 360p + Language Switcher
Default video quality is 360p to save mobile data in rural areas. Audio language can be switched to Urdu/Punjabi/English without reloading. Closed captions available for hearing-impaired users.
→ Req 3.5: Offline/low-data content limitations addressed
5
Quiz: "Hear Answer" Button
Each quiz question has a "🔊 Hear answer" option so users who can't read the explanation can still learn. The correct/wrong visual states use color AND icon — not color alone (colorblind-safe).
→ Req 3.3.3: Voice I/O for illiterate users
6
Completion: "Shabash" + Badges
"Shabash" (شاباش) is culturally familiar Urdu praise. Earned badges and streaks create intrinsic motivation. Gamification is critical for continued engagement in communities where education is not normalized.
→ Req 3.3.4: Engaging learning for rural communities
